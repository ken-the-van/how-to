Campsite

Getting ready to stop for the night

Picking a location

  1. Space
    Check for a location that you are with 10m of the power access point and are level
  2. Access to power
    Before setting up camp check the cable can reach the access point
    The blue connector is under the van just in front of the drivers rear left wheel. Connect to the van and then walk and connect to the campsite outlet
  3. Parking on chocks
    This can be hard due to the weight the van.
    1. Get the chocks from the boot on the left hand side
    2. Put the chocks under the front wheels
    3. Slowly drive up until level or on the top step
    4. Use the foot brake and then pull the handbrake up fully
    5. Leave in gear before releasing the foot brake.
  4. Awning
    Ensure there is a few metres space for the awning from the side of the van if you want to use it

Power Inputs

  1. Solar
    The van has solar panels to keep the leisure battery charged
    This is usually enough for the fridge and charging of phones unless there is a lack of sun exposure or a high power consumption
  2. Hook Up At Campsite
    • A hookup cable is provided in a blue bag in the boot next to the gas safety box
    • The hookup point on the van is under the side of the van just in front of the drivers side back wheel
  3. Hook Up With UK Plug
    You may also hook up to a UK plug socket using the adaptor in the same bag
  4. Hook Up Overseas
    • If travelling overseas you may need to reverse the polarity of the power at a campsite
    • This means adding the reverse polarity cable in between the van socket and the usual cable
    • To know if you need the reverse polarity you can use the red polarity sensor plugged into the multi cable inside the van. It should read 3 green lights and if not do what it says by adapting the reverse polarity cable

Water

  1. Water and Waste Tanks
    In the cupboard beneath the sink are the water tanks There are two tanks one for clean water and one for waste water
  2. Filling Up
    When filling up also empty the waste tank so there is no chance of it overflowing
    1. To fill up Loosen the yellow clip holding the tanks in place
    2. Remove the pump and waste hoses from the top of the tanks
    3. Take the tanks out of the cupboard and go empty and fill at a suitable location
    4. Only fill up the water tank about 2/3rd so that it does not overflow when driving and while putting
    5. the water tanks back in place
    6. Put the tanks back into their locations
    7. Ensure the two hoses are back in the correct locations
    8. Reattach the yellow clips
    9. We also stuff a plastic bag around the top of the fresh water tank to avoid it spilling while driving

Shade

  1. Awning
    You can attach the awning to the side of van to create shade
    1. Retrieve the awning from the boot
    2. Unwrap and assemble to two poles
    3. Locate the ends with the points which go into the awning hoops
    4. Open the side door of the van and slide the awning into the awning rail above the door
    5. The awning can be moved until it covers the side of the van
    6. Put the poles into the hoops and feet on the ground
    7. Peg the guy ropes to the ground or tie them to a suitable hold
    If very windy you will not be able to use the awning
