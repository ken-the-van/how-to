Food and Drink

Preparing and enjoying meals

Food Storage

  1. Non Perishable Food
    We suggest the cupboards oppositve the kitchen
  2. Bottles
    We suggest wine and beers under the cab passenger seats or rear passenger seats if not using child seats
  3. Coffee / Tea
    We suggest using the cupboard above the table
  4. Fresh Food
    Use the fridge under the sink

Fridge

  1. Opening
    Squeeze the handle to unlock and then pull forward to open. Make sure the child harness is out of the way first.
  2. Closing
    Push the door close after checking that all the shelves are closed
  3. Temperature
    The setting is on the inside left of the door. The lowest setting is enough when it has been running for a while. You can adjust to cool quicker but put back to the lowest setting afterwards to avoid draining the battery.

Taps

  1. Pre-checks
    Ensure the tap is off, the sink cover is off and the tap points to the sink. Also that the waste tank is connected up.
  2. Switching on and off
    Open the cupboard next to the fridge. Flick the switch on the right hand side and you will hear the pump activate
  3. Using the tap
    The lever for the tap is just on and off, it doesn’t change the water temperature
  4. Sink cover
    For more worktop space use the sink cover and rotate the tap out the way
  5. Empty Tank
    If the pump just makes noise and no water comes through the tank needs refilling

Water Tanks

  1. Identification
    There are two tanks one marked for 'Water' and one for 'Waste'. Only use the 'Water' tank for clean water and the 'Waste' for the used water
  2. Location
    In the cupboard beneath the hob
  3. Filling Up
    To fill up do the following
    1. Loosen the yellow clip holding the tanks in place
    2. Remove the pump and waste hoses from the top of the tanks
    3. Take the tanks out of the cupboard and go empty and fill at a suitable location
    4. Only fill up the water tank about 2/3rd so that it does not overflow when driving and while putting the water tanks back in place
    5. Put the tanks back into their locations
    6. Ensure the two hoses are back in the correct locations
    7. Reattach the yellow clips
    8. We also stuff a plastic bag around the top of the fresh water tank to avoid it spilling while driving

Hot Water

  1. Gas Kettle
    A gas heated kettle is available in the cupboards above the sink
  2. Kettle
    A small electric kettle that can be used on hookup. Only use this kettle on the power outlets on the side of the kitchen behind the passenger seats since this comes of the hookup power and not the leisure battery

Cooking on the Hob

  1. Utensils
    There are cooking utensils in the right most cupboard above the kitchen
  2. Enabling the Gas
    To use the hob the gas needs to be turned on
    1. The gas cylinder is located in the left hand side of the boot
    2. There is a key to unlock the gas safety box on the van keychain
    3. Unlock the gas safety box, open the door and turn the valve clockwise to open.
    4. Once finished close and lock the gas safety box
    Please turn off the gas when moving the van and driving to be as safe as possible
  3. Using the Hob
    1. With the gas turned on hold down and turn one of the hob knobs and ignite the gas with the lighter provided
    2. The hob is powerful so you will want to start with the minimum setting to avoid burning anything
    3. The cork heat mats are under the sink to be used for any hot pans on the worktop or tables
  4. Ventilation
    It is a good idea to open one of the bed vent windows and then set ceiling fan above the table to extract mode. This will draw air up and out the roof and help avoid the van smelling like your dinner all night

Washing Up

  1. Campsite Facilities
    We advise using campsite facilities to do your main meal washing up as there is more space
  2. Sink
    But there is are tea towels washing liquid and kitchen spray in the kitchen cupboard to wash up in the sink
