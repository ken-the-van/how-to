Interior

Getting to know your mobile home and setting up for a great night's sleep.

Power Usage

  1. Power Levels
    The van display the power level below the bed next to the kitchen
    • This should read 13v or 12v after some use and before hooking up at a campsite
    • If it reads 11v or lower you need to hook up and charge the leisure battery
    • This can be done by connecting the external hook up to a UK socket or campsite socket
  2. Main Power
    A mains outlet is only available when the van is on hook up
    • This plug socket is behind the rear seats on the side of the kitchen
    • A extension cable is plugged in to make this easier to access
  3. 12V Leisure Battery
    The leisure battery serves the following
    • Fridge
    • Kitchen pump
    • Lighting
    • 2 USB sockets are under the table
    • 2 USB sockets are in the bedside cavity on the bottom bunk
    • 2 USB sockets are in the bedside cavity on the top bunk
  4. Circuit Breaker and Fuses
    If the power is unavailable you can check the circuit breaker and fuses in the boot
    • The breaker is right at the back of the centre boot space
    • The fuses are located next to the battery and require the unscrewing of the right side pod to access the electronics area. If you need to do this get in touch with us.

Lighting

  1. Overhead lights
    The ceiling lights can be switched on with the light switch inside the top cupboard opposite the kitchen. This is a dimmable light using the touchscreen to change the brightness

  2. Side lights
    There are a set of USB lights plugged into the top bunk USB sockets that can be used to have a dimmer light in the kitchen and dining area The is a USB light in the lower bunk for bedtime reading

Privacy

  1. Blinds
    Unwrap and use the blinds to cover the cab windows from the inside

Cooling

  1. Ceiling Fans
    Use the ceiling fans to cool the van
    • Press the on/off button to turn each fan on and off
    • The fans will open and close automatically when on and off
    • To manually open the vents (but without the fans) twist the black knobs manually
  2. Side Vents
    Use the two vent windows to cool the rear

    At night opening these and turning the ceiling fans to extract will create a cooling draft throughout the van

Heating

  1. Diesel Heater
    The Diesel Heater can be used to heat up the van
    • Use the control panel beneath the bed to turn on the heater and adjust the temperature
    • There is a button to turn on the heater and to adjust the temperature
    • Once the van is warmer turn off the heater to save the diesel
    The diesel level will be checked and topped up and it will not run out over the course of a week.

    But if you did need to top it up:
    • The fuel is contained under the left hand side pod in the boot
    • It requires carefully filling up with a jug or funnel
    Hopefully this is always topped up and you never have to. Please get in touch if you have any questions as it's important not to spill any fuel

Storage

  1. Shelving
    There are 4 shelving units opposite the kitchen. Ideal for food and clothes
  2. Over the cab
    A sizeable storage area is above the cab. Ideal for clothes and soft items
  3. Under the passenger seats
    Both the front and rear passenger seats have cavity storage space. Ideal for drinks or food. If children’s seats are in use then access is restricted
  4. In the boot
    An area under the bed is available for storage. Ideal for activity items
  5. On the top bed
    When on the move camp chairs and soft items can be stored on the top bed

Dining

  1. Interior Table
    1. The front 3 seats can all be swivelled 180 degrees so that a 5 seater dining setup can be created around the table
    2. First undo the 8 bolts under the seats that connect the seats to the base and place them on the shelf above the passenger seat for safe keeping
    3. Now the seats cab swivel around by hand
    4. It it easier to do this if you open the doors to the cab on both sides as it gives more room to turn the seats
    5. You will need to move the seatbelts as you go and reverse back the other direction when you put the seats back to avoid the seatbelts being twisted
    6. The drivers side can be trickier as you have to contend with the handbrake.
      You can slide the drivers seat forward or backwards to make sure it is central again to be able to swivel the seat

Bedding

  1. Accessing the top bunk
    Use the stairs opposite the kitchen
  2. Securing children
    Use the cargo net by attaching it to the roof hoops in order to add a safety net to those sleeping up top
  3. Blackout blind
    Use the blackout blink and drape over the curtain rail to cover the whole gap in the bedroom
  4. Raising the Bed
    1. The top bed is hinged and so can be raised up to provide additional head room for sitting up on the bottom bed
    2. There are 2 hoops on the roof and two hoops just under the mattress of the top bed which can be seen by slightly folding back the mattress
    3. To raise the bed it needs to be lifted up and attached to the rings in the roof with the heavy duty carabiner clips
    4. As the top bed is rather heavy this is typically a two person job.
    5. One person lifts up (or lays on the bottom bunk and uses their legs) the top bunk to the roof. The other person attaches the carabiners to the roof loops
    6. It’s easier to do the hoop at the front of the van and then go through the rear doors to do the hoop near the rear.
    7. It’s quite comfortable to chill in the bed without raising it but this is another option to try out

Troubleshooting

    Too Dark
    Fix
    Use the USB light in the side pods to have something to use to read
    Too Light
    Fix
    Fill the vent windows with the additional small cushions and then use the blackout blind and hang it over the curtain rail above the top bed. It will fill the gap and provide darkness to the whole bed
    Too Cold
    Fix
    The heater can be used to warm up the van and then extra blankets to keep warm until morning
    Too Hot
    Fix
    Set a ceiling fan to draw the air out the van, then open the vent windows slightly, this will create a draft that pulls cool are over you while you sleep
