Interior

Getting to know your mobile home and setting up for a great night's sleep.

Power Usage

  1. Power Levels
    The van display the power level below the bed next to the kitchen
    • This should read 13v or 12v after some use and before hooking up at a campsite
    • If it reads 11v or lower you need to hook up and charge the leisure battery
  2. Main Power
    A mains outlet is only available when the van is on hook up
    • This plug socket is behind the rear seats on the side of the kitchen
    • A extension cable is plugged in to make this easier to access
  3. 12V Leisure Battery
    The leisure battery serves the following
    • Fridge
    • Kitchen pump
    • Lighting
    • 2 USB sockets are under the table
    • 2 USB sockets are in the bedside cavity on the bottom bunk
    • 2 USB sockets are in the bedside cavity on the top bunk
  4. Circuit Breaker and Fuses
    If the power is unavailable you can check the circuit breaker and fuses in the boot
    • The breaker is right at the back of the centre boot space
    • The fuses are located next to the battery and require the unscrewing of the right side pod to access the electronics area

Lighting

  1. Overhead lights
    The ceiling lights can be switched on with the light switch inside the top cupboard opposite the kitchen. This is a dimmable light using the touchscreen to change the brightness
  2. Side lights
    There are a set of USB lights plugged into the top bunk USB sockets that can be used to have a dimmer light in the kitchen and dining area The is a USB light in the lower bunk for bedtime reading

Privacy

  1. Blinds
    Unwrap and use the blinds to cover the cab windows from the inside

Cooling

  1. Ceiling Fans
    Use the ceiling fans to cool the van
    • Press the on/off button to turn each fan on and off
    • The fans will open and close automatically when on and off
    • To manually open the vents (but without the fans) twist the black knobs manually

Heating

  1. Diesel Heater
    A diesel heater can be used to heat up the van
    • Use the control panel beneath the bed to turn on the heater and adjust the temperature
    • There is a button to turn on the heater and to adjust the temperature
    • Once the van is warmer turn off the heater to save the diesel
    The diesel level will be checked and topped up and it will not run out over the course of a week. But if you did need to top it up, the fuel is contained under the left hand side pod in the boot and requires carefully filling up with a jug or funnel

Storage

  1. Shelving
    There are 4 shelving units opposite the kitchen. Ideal for food and clothes
  2. Over the cab
    A sizeable storage area is above the cab. Ideal for clothes and soft items
  3. Under the passenger seats
    Both the front and rear passenger seats have cavity storage space. Ideal for drinks or food. If children’s seats are in use then access is restricted
  4. In the boot
    An area under the bed is available for storage. Ideal for activity items
  5. On the top bed
    When on the move camp chairs and soft items can be stored on the top bed
